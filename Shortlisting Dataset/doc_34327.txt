Question for written answer E-001526/2011 to the Commission Rule 117 Niki Tzavela (EFD) (23 February 2011) Subject: Egypt — Freedom of the press According to information published on the website ‘kathimerini.gr’, at least six journalists and press photographers were arrested in recent days in Egypt, while covering the demonstrations against the regime. Two of them have since been released. One journalist working for the daily Kathimerini , Petros Papakonstantinou, who was in Egypt to cover the events, was also beaten up by pro-government demonstrators. Furthermore, his passport was confiscated by the authorities, and he has not been authorised to leave the hospital, in which he received tardy first-aid treatment. What measures does the Commission propose to take in order to guarantee the protection of the rights of European journalists and the freedom of the press in those North African and Middle Eastern countries which are currently experiencing unrest?
