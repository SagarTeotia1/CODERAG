19 April 2002 FINAL A5-0125/2002 REPORT on supplementing the Rules of Procedure by a list of Parliament documents directly accessible through the register (2002/2055 REG)) Committee on Constitutional Affairs Rapporteur: Hanja Maij-Weggen 313.378 CONTENTS PROCEDURAL PAGE PROPOSAL FOR A DECISION EXPLANATORY STATEMENT PROCEDURAL PAGE At the sitting of 14 March 2002 the President of Parliament announced that, by letter of 11 December 2001, his predecessor had referred to the Committee on Constitutional Affairs the question of drawing up a report supplementing to the Rules of Procedure by the list, provided for in Rule 172(3) thereof, of categories of Parliament documents directly accessible to the public through the register of Parliament documents. (2002/2055(REG)). At its meeting of 20 February 2002 the Committee on Constitutional Affairs appointed Hanja Maij-Weggen rapporteur. The committee considered the draft report at its meetings of 27 March 2002 and 18 April 2002. At the latter/last meeting it adopted the proposal for a decision by 28 votes to 1 with 2 abstentions. The following were present for the vote: Giorgio Napolitano, chairman; Jo Leinen, vice-chairman and Ursula Schleicher, vice-chairman; Hanja Maij-Weggen, rapporteur; Teresa Almeida Garrett, Enrique Barón Crespo, Margrietus J. van den Berg (for Dimitris Tsatsos), Georges Berthus, Guido Bodrato (for Luigi Ciriaco De Mita), Jens-Peter Bonde, Carlos Carnero González, Richard Corbett, Paolo Costa, Jean-Maurice Dehousse, Gianfranco Dell’Alba (for Olivier Dupuis), Giorgos Dimitrakopoulos, Andrew Nicholas Duff, Olivier Duhamel, Monica Frassoni, José María Gil-Robles Gil-Delgado, Gerhard Hager, Daniel J. Hannan, Sylvia-Yvonne Kaufmann, Alain Lamassoure (for François Bayrou), Neil MacCormick (for Johannes Voggenhuber), Luís Marinho, Hans-Peter Martin, Iñigo Méndez de Vigo, Gérard Onesta, José Ribeiro e Castro (for Mariotto Segni) and Antonio Tajani. The report was tabled on 19 April 2002. The deadline for tabling amendments will be indicated in the draft agenda for the relevant part-session.
