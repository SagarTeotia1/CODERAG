Answer given by Mrs Reding on behalf of the Commission (12 July 2006) The Commission appreciates the interest of the Honourable Member in its work regarding the opening of the 2 gigahertz (GHz) band for mobile television services and the importance of the development of mobile television and other new and innovative services in the information society this opening would make possible. The Commission is working with the responsible national authorities and the European Conference of Postal and Telecommunications Administrations (CEPT) towards the elaboration of a stable and coordinated regulatory framework to make this possible in the European Union. The Commission acknowledges that mobile television platforms combining satellite and terrestrial platforms could be an interesting solution to offer Europe wide mobile television services Providing both coordination and legal certainity for the use of the 2 GHz band, presupposes to consider all interests at stake. Ongoing discussions relate in particular to both the coordination of licensing and selection procedures for the authorisation of systems which could operate in all Member States. It also covers the harmonised allocation of the necessary radio spectrum in the 2GHz band for which a draft Commission decision will soon be discussed in the Radio Spectrum Committee pursuant to the Radio Spectrum Decision No 676/2002/EC Decision No 676/2002/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 7 March 2002 on a regulatory framework for radio spectrum policy in the European Community (Radio Spectrum Decision), OJ L 108, 24.4.2002. , with the aim to adopt the decision by the end of 2006.
