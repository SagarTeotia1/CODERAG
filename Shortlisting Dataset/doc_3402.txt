WRITTEN QUESTION E-0585/09 by Frank Vanhecke (NI) to the Commission (9 February 2009) Subject: Terrorist threat involving biological weapons Islamist terrorists are known to be working on developing all sorts of biological weapons such as deadly contagious diseases, etc. Dr Igor Khrupinov, an expert at Georgia University in the USA, has pointed out the dangers for Europe. Infected terrorists walking around European cities could cause more victims than bomb attacks. It was reported that in 2008 100 Islamist terrorists had attempted to become postgraduate students in Great Britain to get use of laboratories (‘Al-Quaeda cell killed by Black Death “was developing biological weapons” ’, Telegraph.co.uk , 20 January 2009). What measures has the Commission taken to prevent such terrorist attacks?
