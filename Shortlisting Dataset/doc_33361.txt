WRITTEN QUESTION E-2793/05 by Antonios Trakatellis (PPE-DE) and Georgios Papastamkos (PPE-DE) to the Commission (26 July 2005) Subject: Nuclear safety and public health protection in FYROM: construction of nuclear waste facility and unsupervised landfill A written complaint has been submitted by a non-governmental organisation and local residents of Skopje and the surrounding area regarding the planned construction of a nuclear waste facility in Petrovec. Under Article 103 of the Stabilisation and Association Agreement between the European Communities and their Member States of the one part and the former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia of the other part, FYROM Council Decision and Commission Decision 2004/239/EC, Euratom, OJ L 84, 20.3.2004 . is required to cooperate in the field of nuclear safety, while the adoption of environmental and waste management legislation is included among the priorities and conditions for European partnership arrangements with FYROM Council Decision 2004/518/EC, OJ L 222, 23.6.2004 . . In view of the public health hazards and cross-border pollution in Greece and Bulgaria arising from such landfill sites: 1. Have the Commission, the Association Council or other responsible bodies been informed of the construction of the nuclear waste disposal facility and what information does the Commission have regarding the uncontrolled radioactive waste landfill in FYROM? 2. What undertakings must be made by the FYROM authorities and what requirements must be met for the location of such a hazardous facility near the border with an EU Member State (Greece)? 3. Has the FYROM adopted environmental and nuclear waste management legislation under the partnership arrangements and what steps will the Commission ensure compliance by FYROM with its obligations under the Stabilisation and Association Agreement? 4. Is public health protection a mandatory requirement in respect of the application by FYROM for accession to the EU?
