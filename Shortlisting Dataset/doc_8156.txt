According to Article 27 (2) of the Audiovisual Media Services Directive Directive 2010/13/EU of the Parliament and of the Council of 10 March 2010 on the coordination of certain provisions laid down by law, regulation or administrative action in Member States concerning the provision of audiovisual media services (Audiovisual Media Services Directive), OJ L 95, 15.4.2010 . TV programmes which are likely to impair the physical, mental or moral development of minors may be transmitted when it is ensured, by selecting the time of the broadcast or by any technical measure (e.g. encryption), that minors in the area of transmission will not normally hear or see such broadcasts. This provision must be implemented in each Member State and construed in line with the standards set by the Court of Justice of the European Union. It is up to the Member States and their competent regulatory bodies to define, in the first instance, which content is likely to impair the physical, mental or moral development of minors.
