On Monday and Tuesday: Slovenian ministers will present the priorities of their presidency of the EU to the Parliament's committees. What does a committee do? Considers and deals with reports and opinions. Normally drafts of legislation and other texts from the Council and the Commission. 20: number of permanent committees 2: subcommittees - human rights and security and defence 1: Temporary committee - currently on climate change. How are committee members chosen? The d'Hondt proportional representation system - the bigger the group the more seats they get on committees. Who are the key people? Each committee has a chair and 4 vice-chairs. Reports are prepared by "rapporteurs" who are chosen by the Committee. They: draw up the draft report presenting Parliament's position on the subject in question, submit the text to their committee, take account of committee members' views and incorporate amendments adopted by the committee, lead negotiations with other EU institutions and other third parties present report in plenary. Where do they meet? Mainly Brussels but sometimes in Strasbourg How busy are they? Between 1999 and 2004 they produced 2077 reports.
