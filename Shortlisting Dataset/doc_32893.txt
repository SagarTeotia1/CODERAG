23.10.2006 MOTION FOR A RESOLUTION pursuant to Rule 103(2) of the Rules of Procedure by Annemie Neyts-Uyttebroeck and Henrik Lax on behalf of the ALDE Group on Georgia (South Ossetia) European Parliament resolution on Georgia (South Ossetia) The European Parliament , – having regard to the Partnership and Cooperation Agreement between the European Union and Georgia, which entered into force on 1 July 1999, – having regard to the Partnership and Cooperation Agreement between the European Union and the Russian Federation, which entered into force on 1 December 1997, – having regard to its report of 26 February 2004 on EU policy towards the South Caucasus and its report of May 2005 on EU-Russia relations, – having regard to its previous resolutions on Georgia, in particular that of 14 October 2004, and its resolution of 19 January 2006 on the European Neighbourhood Policy, – having regard to the conclusions of the General Affairs and External Relations Council of 17 October 2006, – having regard to Rule 103(2) of its Rules of Procedure, A. whereas the recent increase in tensions between Georgia and Russia is harming efforts to find a peaceful solution to the frozen conflicts in Georgia and is detrimental to the normalisation of relations between Georgia and Russia and to the overall stability of the region, B. whereas this increased tension follows the detention in Georgia of four Russian military officers, accused of espionage, who have now been handed over and returned to Russia, C. whereas there have been signs of growing tension for some time, in particular in South Ossetia, D. whereas the Russian response to the arrest of the four military officers was heavy-handed and disproportionate and included recalling its ambassador to Tbilisi, a freezing of transport and postal communications and the imposition of other sanctions on Georgia, leading to a deterioration in Georgian-Russian relations and to a sharp increase in the tensions in South Ossetia, E. whereas following a decision by the South Ossetia leadership a referendum on self-determination will take place, together with presidential elections, on 12 November 2006, F. whereas there can be no lasting solution to the conflicts except through negotiations, G. whereas the established negotiation mechanisms - the quadripartite negotiations for South Ossetia and the so-called Geneva mechanism for Abkhazia - have been criticised for having so far failed to bring the parties closer to a peaceful settlement of the conflicts, H. whereas Russia has signalled that attempts to move towards independence for Kosovo, a province of Serbia administered by the United Nations since 1999, would be seen by it as a precedent that could have implications in Georgia as well as in the wider Caucasus region, I. whereas the European Union should increase its support for the efforts to resolve the frozen conflicts in Georgia and the South Caucasus region, in cooperation with OSCE, the UN and all other actors, 1.
