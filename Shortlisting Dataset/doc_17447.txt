Answer given by Mrs de Palacio on behalf of the Commission (24 July 2003) With regard to the current state of the accession negotiations on the issue of nuclear safety in units 3 and 4 of the Kozloduy power station, the Commission would remind the Honourable Members that, in the framework of the Accession Conference, Chapter 14 (Energy) was provisionally closed with Bulgaria on 18 November 2002. The terms of the agreement between the Member States and Bulgaria on nuclear safety, in particular as regards units 3 and 4 of the Kozloduy power station, are set out in the EU common position. The Bulgarian Government has undertaken to close units 3 and 4 of the Kozloduy power station in 2006. As regards the peer review under the auspices of the Council, the European Union will send an expert mission to Bulgaria as soon as possible, before the conclusion of the accession negotiations. The EU also notes Bulgaria's expectation that the Community will continue to provide financial assistance (linked to the consequences of the early closure of certain units of the Kozloduy power station) and is willing to consider this matter. The Commission considers that the questions raised by the Honourable Members concerning units 3 and 4 of the Kozloduy power station must be understood and reassessed in this framework. The Commission would point out that, like all the other nuclear installations in the candidate countries, units 3 and 4 of the Kozloduy power station were evaluated by the Working Party on Nuclear Safety (WPNS) under the auspices of the Council. The evaluation gave rise to recommendations, and the WPNS monitored the implementation of those recommendations in the first half of 2002. The WPNS considered that the implementation of five recommendations required further close monitoring, and in its reports of June 2001 and 2002 it stressed that the improvements made to units 3 and 4 should be understood in the context of the units being closed in 2006.
