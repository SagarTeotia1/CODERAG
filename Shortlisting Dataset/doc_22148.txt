Notes that this resolution is based on the results of the first five conformity decisions taken by the Commission under the reformed procedure, and stresses that future conformity decisions might give rise to new considerations; 2. Takes the view that the relatively limited experience gained to date with the reformed clearance procedure prompts a certain prudence in the evaluation; recognises, nonetheless, that the clearance of accounts procedure is a mechanism that provides for the systematic control of Community expenditure; 3. Notes that the Court of Auditors concludes that the reform of the clearance procedure has resulted in improved accountability at Member State level and should be regarded as a considerable achievement OJ C 69, 2.3.2001, p. 4. ; 4. Notes with disappointment that, whilst for Parliament one of the main objectives of the reform was to avoid delays in the clearance decisions, the Court of Auditors has not found evidence that the reformed procedure has accelerated the timescale for clearing overall expenditure for a given year Special Report 22/2000, point 90. ; From annual to multiannual clearance decisions 5. Notes that decisions taken as a result of the compliance clearance, being either a deduction from or an addition to the Community financing, will each time relate to specific budget areas and will usually cover the expenditure of more than one financial year; 6. Notes that the Court of Auditors, in its opinion 5/1994 OJ C 383, 31.12.1994, p. 31. , confirmed its preference for a single, annual clearance decision, whilst at the same time recognising the problems associated with the old system (long delays in the clearance decisions) and also considered the split into an annual accounting clearance and a multiannual compliance clearance to be a compromise rather than a solution to the problems; 7.
