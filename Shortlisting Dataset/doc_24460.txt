16.11.2009 B7-0140/2009 MOTION FOR A RESOLUTION pursuant to Rule 120 of the Rules of Procedure in support of installing the European Extremely Large Telescope (E‑ELT) on the island of La Palma (Canary Islands) Gabriel Mato Adrover PE428.756v01-00 B7‑0140/2009 Motion for a European Parliament resolution in support of installing the European Extremely Large Telescope (E‑ELT) on the island of La Palma (Canary Islands) The European Parl i ament , – having regard to Rule 120 of its Rules of Procedure, A. whereas the Roque de los Muchachos Observatory on the island of La Palma (Canary Islands) is one of the three leading observatories in the world, B. whereas the Gran Telescopio Canarias (Canaries Great Telescope), whose primary mirror is over ten metres in diameter, is the largest optical telescope in the world, C. whereas the special climatic conditions at Roque de los Muchachos − high altitude above cloud level, clear field of view and absence of light pollution − make for a pure atmosphere and hence extraordinarily clear astral nights in the skies above La Palma, D. whereas Law 31/1988 (the ‘Sky Law’) protects the quality of the sky in the Canary Islands, E. whereas UNESCO has declared La Palma a biosphere reserve, and whereas its position close to the equator allows the whole of the northern hemisphere and part of the southern hemisphere to be observed, F. whereas La Palma perfectly fulfils the conditions as regards location, time zone, seismic activity, facilities for researchers and the reconciliation of work and family life, G. whereas La Palma is the only European candidate for the siting of this giant telescope, H. whereas the European Organisation for Astronomical Research (ESO) will take its decision on where to site the E-ELT by 2010, 1. Expresses its support for installing the E-ELT on the island of La Palma (Canary Islands).
