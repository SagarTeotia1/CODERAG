MEPs want legislation to enhance individual responsibility of board members and the inclusion of independent directors on a management board in order to guarantee better auditing. Parliament also wants more involvement of employees and calls for the presence of representatives of employees on management or supervisory boards in order to enable the employees to safeguard their interests in connection with decisions directly impacting firms' future. Other suggestions include legislation to improve companies' auditing by forcing them to rotate their audit firm or the audit partner in charge of their accounts, and to prohibit auditors from providing non-audit services to audit clients. In any case, MEPs underline that the response taken to the current situation should be proportionate, balanced and sensible, and take into account the needs of participants in the financial markets. A proper, full assessment should be carried out on what happened at Parmalat. As for the measures taken by the Italian government after the Parmalat affair, Parliament wants the Commission to decide as soon as possible whether they are compatible with EC law. At the plenary debate on 11 February, Frits BOLKENSTEIN , the Commissioner for Internal Market, Taxation and Customs Union issues, assured Parliament of the commitment of the Commission to develop proposals to bring order to the current situation on the financial market. Later this year, the Commission will present legislation in the following areas: the role of non-executive directors on the boards, directors' responsibility for company accounts, and offshore structures and money laundering. Parmalat, a major player in European food business and an employer of tens of thousands of people world-wide, was declared officially insolvent by an Italian court after a big hole was revealed in its accounts.
