WRITTEN QUESTION E-4727/06 by Daniel Varela Suanzes-Carpegna (PPE‑DE) to the Commission (6 November 2006) Subject: The Probo Koala With regard to the Panamanian flag flying, Greek-owned and Dutch-chartered Probo Koala : 1. Is it true that this vessel dumped over 400 tonnes of toxic waste in the harbour of Abiyán (Côte d'Ivoire), resulting in eight deaths and affecting some 85000 people? 2. What kind of toxic products were involved, and what caused the deaths and injuries involved? 3. Did this vessel enter any European port, and specifically, any Spanish port? 4. If so, was it inspected by the port authorities? What was the result? Was the Commission informed? 5. Did the vessel's cargo meet the legal requirements laid down by Community law? 6. What court proceedings have been opened, both within the EU and elsewhere, in connection with this case, and has the Commission appeared in these court proceedings? 7. Is this vessel the twin of the Probo Emu , belonging to the same company, and are both vessels used for transporting the same kind of cargos?
