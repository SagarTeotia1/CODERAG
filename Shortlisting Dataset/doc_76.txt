8.12.2004 MOTION FOR A RESOLUTION to wind up the debate on the statement by the Commission pursuant to Rule 103(2) of the Rules of Procedure by Martin Schulz and Hannes Swoboda on behalf of the PSE Group on the Commission's strategic policy guidelines European Parliament resolution on the Commission's strategic policy guidelines The European Parliament , – having regard to its resolution of 18 November 2004 on the election of the new Commission, – having regard to the current framework agreement on relations between the European Parliament and the Commission, approved by the Conference of Presidents on 29 June 2000, – having regard to Rule 103(2) of its Rules of Procedure, A. whereas it is imperative that at the start of its term of office the Commission should submit general policy guidelines on which it will base its strategy during the five years ahead, and on the basis of which it will draw up its successive annual legislative programmes, B. whereas the Commission proposals should constitute a real roadmap enabling Parliament to monitor, assess and judge the results achieved, C. recalling the commitments given by the Commission on legislative programming and the associated timetable approved by the Conference of Presidents on 30 January 2002, D. recalling that at the hearings of Commissioners-designate, a number of specific commitments were given by the latter on outstanding issues or new Commission proposals and expecting those commitments to be faithfully reflected in the Commission's work and legislative programme, 1. Points out firstly that speedy ratification of the European Constitution will provide the stable political framework within which our policy objectives can be realised; in this context, stresses the need for an appropriate campaign and communications strategy to be devised; 2. Considers that the Commission's policy agenda for the period 2005-2009 should pursue three essential objectives: - promoting the European economic and social model within the framework of sustainable development, full employment and cohesion, - developing an area of freedom, security and justice, - affirming Europe's place in the world; 3. Calls on the Commission to expand on each of these objectives through an operational programme and practical proposals; Promoting the European economic and social model within the framework of sustainable development, full employment and social cohesion 4. Affirms that cohesion is a fundamental principle of the Treaties and of the future European Constitution which strengthens relations between Europe, its regions and economic, social and territorial cohesion; reaffirms its position that for the financial instruments for achieving the Union's objectives, including cohesion, should be adequately funded; 5. Calls on the Commission to pursue the objective of strengthening the European model, stressing the interdependence between economic, social and environmental progress; points out that the visions of a stronger economy and a better society are complementary and that Europe's competitiveness strategy for the 21st century must be based on excellence in education, training and research, high-quality public services and infrastructure, social integration, better working conditions, a better work/life balance and a leading role in the world in terms of technologies and environmental standards; 6.
