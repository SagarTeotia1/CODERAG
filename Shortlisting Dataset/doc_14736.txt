WRITTEN QUESTION E-3781/07 by Alexandru-Ioan Mor≈£un (ALDE) to the Commission (20 July 2007) Subject: Community action programme in the field of public health Parliament has adopted the report by Antonios Trakatellis on the second Community action programme in the field of public health. The compromise text proposes reducing inequalities in the Member States' health service systems, promoting crossborder medical care services and encouraging mobility for both patients and professionals in the medical sector. With a view to further developing these important proposals, can the Commission answer the following questions? 1. Who will be responsible for providing the necessary guarantees regarding crossborder medical treatment? 2. In the context of the free movement of persons and to facilitate the access of insurees to treatment requiring advanced and, in general, extremely expensive medical technology (high-performance medical equipment, use of a wide spectrum of drugs and complex medical procedures), it is important to develop and approve common European strategies leading to unified rules. By when might such a strategy be ready? 3. What improvements should be offered by the health service providers and social security bodies responsible for receiving and treating patients, and what ideas could help facilitate crossborder healthcare?
