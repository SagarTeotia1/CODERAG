Is aware that the subsidies granted under this chapter give the Union a public profile and that new beneficiaries should be able to participate; therefore decides that a maximum ceiling of earmarked appropriations should apply to some items on the basis of a global envelope fixed by the European Parliament in the context of the annual budgetary procedure; 47. Draws to the Commission's attention that in general it may raise justified objections to the comments made in the budget concerning earmarking after the European Parliament's first reading, whereas after the European Parliament's second reading, the Commission must implement the budget according to Article 274 of the EC Treaty; 48. Reserves the right to take all appropriate steps as regards its decisions concerning the 2001 budget, in the light of the way in which expenditure entered in the 2000 budget has been effected; 49. Urges the Commission to present proposals for legal bases to continue successful pilot projects and preparatory actions, in particular concerning the preparatory actions, which are in their second and third year, in order to take them into acount in the budget 2001; 50. Instructs its President to forward this resolution to the Council and the Commission.
