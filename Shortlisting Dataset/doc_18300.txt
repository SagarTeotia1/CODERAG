Has decided on the following measures: - reduction of the standard abatement to 3.5%; - deletion of 24 LA6, 4 B4 and 6 C5 posts; - creation of 24 LA7, 10 B5 and 17 C5 temporary posts for the Translation Directorate; - creation of 1 A7, 1 B5 and 1 C5 temporary posts for the Court of First Instance; - creation of 1 A2, 1 A5 and 3 A7 permanent posts for the general administration; - authorisation of 40 upgradings of permanent posts (2 A6 to A5, 1 A7 to A6, 13 LA5 to LA4, 13 LA6 to LA5, 1 B2 to B1, 5 C2 to C1, 4 C3 to C2 and 1 C4 to C3), as the first of three tranches of 40 upgradings each, in order to implement the new promotion policy; - increase in appropriations against Item 1896 ("Supplementary services for the translation department”) by EUR 1 million; 34. Is of the opinion that immediate measures need to be taken to reduce the translation backlog; points out, nevertheless, that this is a temporary problem that does not require permanent posts to be resolved; points out that some of the translators can be recruited at LA7 level; 35. Agrees that the Court cannot influence the number of cases it is called on to consider; calls on the Court, however, to examine further ways to rationalise its working methods and limit the volume of texts that it deems necessary to translate; believes that more flexibility in the choice of working language (especially in the Court of First Instance) could reduce the pressure on the translation service and calls on the Courts to study how the length of certain preparatory documents could be reduced; Section V - Court of Auditors 36.
