Even if all Member States were to provide all required statistics in good time, the cooperation of Member States would still be crucial. Eurostat databases are not meant to include specific information such as the average time taken by a typical firm to fulfil an administrative obligation. Furthermore, this needs to be assessed at national or subnational level. Member States are best placed to produce these specific estimates, while the Commission is best placed to coordinate this work and aggregate/extrapolate (sub)national data.
