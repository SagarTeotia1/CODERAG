Answer given by Mr Frattini on behalf of the Commission (22 August 2006) There is no evidence suggesting that one particular community cannot integrate into European societies and respect European fundamental norms and values. Moreover, as pointed out by the Council of the European Union, ‘it is vital for Member States to maintain and further develop societies in which newcomers feel welcome, which are defined by a spirit of mutual understanding and accommodation, and where there are clear expectations of all residents — new and old’ Council Document 14615/04 of 19 November 2004. . This leads to greater social cohesion, an increased feeling of security and cultural diversity.
