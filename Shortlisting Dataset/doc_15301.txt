31 August 2000 MOTION FOR A RESOLUTION further to the Council and Commission statements pursuant to Rule 37(2) of the Rules of Procedure by Stephen Hughes, Eryl Margaret McNally, Robert Goebbels and Bruno Trentin on behalf of the PSE Group on creating a monitoring centre for industrial change Resolution on creating a monitoring centre for industrial change The European Parliament, - having regard to the 1989 Community Charter of the Fundamental Social Rights of Workers and the relevant provisions of the EC Treaty, in particular Article 136 thereof, which stipulates that the objectives of the Member States shall include the promotion of employment, improved living and working conditions, proper social protection and dialogue between management and labour with a view to lasting high employment and the combating of exclusion, - having regard to Article 81 of the EC Treaty which gives the European Community the objective to prohibit all agreements between undertakings, decisions by associations of undertakings and concerted practices which may affect trade between Member States and which have as their object or effect the prevention, restriction or distortion of competition within the common market; - having regard to the final report of the High-Level Group (so-called 'Gyllenhammar Group'),on the economic and social implications of industrial change, - having regard to the summary note of the EU Portuguese Presidency on the High-level Forum of 15 June 2000 in Brussels on employment, economic reforms and social cohesion, - having regard to the European Social Partners' joint position on an Observatory for Industrial Changes, which was presented at the High Level Forum held in Brussels on 15 June 2000, - having regard to the speech of its President Nicole Fontaine at the Lisbon Summit, on 24 March 2000, and in particular to the idea of “setting up an Observatory of Industrial Change”, -
