WRITTEN QUESTION E-0528/07 by Ilda Figueiredo (GUE/NGL) to the Commission (12 February 2007) Subject: Wage discrimination against women In this European Year of Equal Opportunities for All, the task of carrying out research and collecting data relating to the causes of the earnings differential between men and women and to the action taken (or to be taken) by the Member States in order to eradicate it must be completed, despite what is laid down in Council Directive 75/117/EEC OJ L 45, 19.2.1975, p. 19. of 10 February 1975 on the approximation of the laws of the Member States relating to the application of the principle of equal pay for men and women. In this connection, would the Commission answer the following questions? 1. What stage has been reached in the research referred to above? 2. Have quantitative targets and time limits been laid down in the employment guidelines? 3. Is a report on the subject (including details of the action to be taken) to be drawn up and sent to the European Parliament?
