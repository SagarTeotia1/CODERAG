Answer given by Mr Špidla on behalf of the Commission (7 August 2007) Council Directive 92/57/EEC of 24 June 1992 on the implementation of minimum safety and health requirements at temporary or mobile construction sites (eighth individual Directive within the meaning of Article 16 (1) of Directive 89/391/EEC) OJ L 245, 26.8.1992. was transposed into Irish law by the ‘Safety, Health and Welfare at Work (Construction) Regulations’ 2006 (S.I. No 504 of 2006). It is primarily up to the competent Irish authorities to ensure proper surveillance and control of the implementation of the Irish provisions transposing this directive. In the specific case mentioned by the Honourable Member, control is the responsibility of the ‘Health and Safety Authority’ http://www.hsa.ie to which all information concerning any infringements of the safety and health rules should be addressed.
