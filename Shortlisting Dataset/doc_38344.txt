10 October 2001 MOTION FOR A RESOLUTION pursuant to Rule 48 of the Rules of Procedure by Adriana Poli Bortone on the protection of religious monuments in Kosovo Motion for a European Parliament resolution on the protection of religious monuments in Kosovo The European Parliament, A. whereas many monasteries and Orthodox churches in Kosovo are threatened with destruction by Muslim extremists, B. noting that these religious monuments are seen, in context of the inter-ethnic struggle, as a reminder of the Serb presence, C. whereas the Monastery of the Holy Trinity in Musutiste has already been completely destroyed, whilst the Orthodox monastery of Gracanica, the Cathedral of the Mother of God at Ljevisa, the Church of the Presentation of the Virgin in Lipljan, the Monastery of the Patriarchate of Pec, together with the complex consisting of the churches of the Holy Apostles, Santa Maria Odighitria and San Domenico and Decani Monastery are still at great risk, 1. Calls for European Union intervention in the form of measures to protect and safeguard the sites threatened with destruction; 2. Points out that the NATO peace-keeping forces, committed to protecting the population in Kosovo, should also protect the threatened religious monuments, in order to prevent ethnic hatred from promoting their destruction, as happened with the statues of Buddha in Bamiyan, Afghanistan.
