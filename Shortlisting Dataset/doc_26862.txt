Answer given by Mr Figel’ on behalf of the Commission (23 June 2005) The Commission is not aware of this draft legislation proposing modification of the national law on voluntary activities in Italy. The Commission, however, agrees with the Honourable Member that voluntary work is essential in promoting active citizenship. The Commission would like to underline that legislation on voluntary work exclusively falls within the competence of Member States. However, Article 149 of the EC Treaty provides for Community action to promote mobility of young people. In this context, the Youth programme promotes voluntary service of young people through the ‘European Voluntary Service’ action. In addition, following the Commission’s White Paper on Youth, the Council adopted a resolution in 2002 Resolution of the Council and of the representatives of the governments of the Member States meeting within the Council of 27 June 2002 regarding the framework of European cooperation in the youth field, OJ C 168/2, 13.7.2002. launching an open method of coordination in the field of youth, including a priority on voluntary activities. In this context, the Council adopted common objectives on voluntary activities on 15 November 2004 which aim to promote, facilitate and recognise voluntary activities of young people Resolution of the Council and the representatives of the governments of the Member States meeting within the Council on common objectives for voluntary activities of young people of 15 November 2004, Council Document 13996/04; JEUN 89, EDUC 211, SOC 512. . They also invite Member States to define measures at national level and to present, by the end of 2006, national reports on implementation of the common objectives. On that basis, the Commission will present a progress report to the Council. The Parliament will be informed.
