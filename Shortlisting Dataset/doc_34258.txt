Reaffirms that the adoption of an EU common policy on asylum and immigration is a condition for EU involvement in the management of external borders; firmly believes that the EU borders must remain open to refugees who are fleeing repression in search of a place of asylum in the Member States of the Union; 14. Stresses, therefore, that all the measures concerning external border control, stopping of illegal immigration, VIS, return policy, in particular in the framework of the dialogue with third countries of origin and transit of migratory flows must not undermine the non-refoulement principle and the possibility for an asylum seeker to have access to the asylum procedure; 15. Calls on the Council, in particular, in the context of the return action programme and negotiation of readmission agreements, to counterbalance all the measures already taken on combating illegal immigration with the opening of legal means of immigration in the EU as well as an active policy of integration and promotion of rights of third country nationals residing on the EU territory; 16. Calls in particular for the directives on asylum qualification and procedures - which should have been adopted by the end of this year - to provide for full respect to be shown for the right of access to asylum procedures and to be respectful of the Geneva Convention and its protocol as well as the UNHCR handbook; 17. Calls on the Council to adopt without delay the Directive on the temporary residence permit issued to third-country nationals victims of trafficking in human beings or to third-country nationals who have been subject of an action to facilitate illegal immigration who cooperate with the competent authorities; Judicial and Police Cooperation 18. Calls once again on the Council - as regards the European Council's call for the competent authorities of the Member States to make more effective use of the existing instrument of judicial and police cooperation, including joint investigation teams - to adopt a framework decision on common standards governing procedural law as a condition for the entering into force of the European arrest warrant; 20.
