Answer given by Mr Tajani on behalf of the Commission (4 September 2009) Regulation (EC) No 561/2006 Regulation (EC) No 561/2006 of Parliament and of the Council of 15 March 2006 on the harmonisation of certain social legislation relating to road transport and amending Council Regulations (EEC) No 3821/85 and (EC) No 2135/98 and repealing Council Regulation (EEC) No 3820/85, OJ L 102, 11.4.2006. on driving times and rest periods and Article 4(4) of its implementing Directive 2006/22/EC Directive 2006/22/EC of Parliament and of the Council of 15 March 2006 on minimum conditions for the implementation of Council Regulations (EEC) No 3820/85 and (EEC) No 3821/85 concerning social legislation relating to road transport activities and repealing Council Directive 88/599/EEC, OJ L 102, 11.4.2006. clearly provide for non-discrimination during the course of roadside checks. On the basis of Article 17 of Regulation (EC) No 561/2006, the Commission publishes a report every two years concerning the application of the regulation, based upon the information provided by the Member States. The most recent report covers the period between 1 January 2005 and 31 December 2006 and was adopted by the Commission on 3 August 2009 COM(2009)416 final. . The said report highlights among other things the Commission's that in some Member States attention needs to be paid to the respect of the non-discrimination principle when checking heavy goods vehicles. In order to improve the quality of the reporting and in accordance with Article 17 of Regulation (EC) No 561/2006, the Commission has drawn up a standard reporting form for Member States Commission decision of 22 September 2008 drawing up the standard reporting form referred to in Article 17 of Regulation (EC) No 561/2006, COM(2008)5123 final. . With this form, detailed information on roadside checks will in future become available according to the country of registration of the vehicles checked. This will allow an in-depth verification of compliance with the non-discrimination requirement and is expected to prevent unfair vehicle checks in the future.
