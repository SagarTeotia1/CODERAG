Member States should already prosecute child pornography on the Internet as far as they are obliged to do so in accordance with the Council Framework Decision of 22 December 2003 on combating the sexual exploitation of children and child pornography OJ L 13, 20.1.2004. . Member States had to implement the framework Decision by 20 January 2006. Currently, the Commission is concerned with the evaluation of the implementation and is about to prepare a report thereon. Moreover, the Safer Internet plus programme aims to promote safer use of the Internet and new online technologies, particularly for children, and to fight against illegal content and content unwanted by the end-user, as part of a coherent approach by the European Union. Inter alia , two networks with national presence are funded under the Safer Internet programme: Inhope, which allows members of the public to report illegal content on the Internet, and Insafe, which carries out awareness actions and runs websites to inform parents, children and teachers on the safe use of Internet. More detailed information is availabe on the website of the Commission, Directorate-General Information Society and Media.
