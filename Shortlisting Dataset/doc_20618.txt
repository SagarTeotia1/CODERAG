The Member States remain the decision-makers with respect to their internal language policy, including regional and minority languages, for which the Council of Europe's European Charter for Regional or Minority Languages provides a comprehensive framework. This framework is not part of Community law. Finally, anyone who considers that any of her or his fundamental rights have been violated may lodge a complaint with the Council of Europe's European Court of Human Rights. That Court may only deal with a matter after all domestic remedies have been exhausted.
