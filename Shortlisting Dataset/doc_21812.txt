Answer given by Mr Michel on behalf of the Commission (12 June 2009) As detailed in the annex, sent directly to the Honourable Member and to Parliament's Secretariat, the EC cooperation with Mozambique during the period 2004‑09 is estimated at EUR 786 million in terms of global commitments financed by the European Development Fund (EDF) and the Budget. This includes an amount of EUR 433 million recently decided by the Commission (December 2008) for which the implementation will start in 2009. In addition to the total envelope, an amount of EUR 11.6 million has been committed to support humanitarian interventions during the period 2004‑09. EC cooperation with Mozambique currently encompasses 2 National Indicative Programmes (NIP) of the 9th and 10th EDF. NIP of the 10th EDF with an initial allocation of EUR 662 million (not including PALOP (Pays Africains de Langue Officielle Portugaise) initiatives, see Annex A) was signed in December 2007. The focal areas of EC support under the 10th EDF or main areas of intervention are: Macroeconomic Support, Transport Infrastructures and Agriculture & Rural development. Other non focal areas of intervention include Health and HIV/AIDS, Good Governance and Non-State Actors.
