Under the eCommerce Directive Directive 2000/31/EC of Parliament and of the Council of 8 June 2000 on certain legal aspects of information society services, in particular electronic commerce, in the internal market (‘Directive on electronic commerce’), OJ L 178, 17.7.2000. web hosting service providers, when they obtain knowledge of illegal activities, are obliged to disable access to the content concerned. 5. In its communication on fighting spam, spyware and malicious software COM(2006)688 final. , the Commission has called on Member States to step up their efforts against these problems. It has also recognised the need for close cooperation between enforcement authorities, network operators and ISPs for the exchange of information and technical expertise. Service providers should ensure that their customers are fully informed about the risks and the possibilities to protect themselves. The Article 29 Working Party on Data Protection has issued an opinion providing guidance on the application of e-mail screening techniques in compliance with legal obligations. The Commission has called on service providers to apply an e-mail filtering policy compliant with this guidance. As stated in its communication ‘Towards a general policy on the fight against cyber crime’ COM(2007)267 final. , the Commission is committed to promoting the development of technical methods and procedures to fight fraud and illegal trade on the Internet, including through public-private cooperation projects.
