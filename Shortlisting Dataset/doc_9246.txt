Is deeply concerned at the unexplained disappearance of journalists, and urgently calls on the governments of the countries concerned to do everything possible to discover their whereabouts, 5. Stresses the need for independence and impartiality of the judiciary, pursuant to the resolution of the United Nations Commission on Human Rights, 6. Calls on the governments of the countries concerned to ensure that the journalists who have been detained are given a fair trial on the basis of a thorough and impartial enquiry in line with international norms; 7. Therefore calls for these trials to be held in public and for the presence of international observers to be authorised at the opening and throughout the whole course of the trial, 8. In this context, deplores the attacks on the diversity and freedom of the press in Russia and calls on the competent authorities to do everything possible to re-establish press freedom in that country, 9. Instructs its President to forward this resolution to the ACP-EU Council, the United Nations Security Council and the governments of the countries concerned.
