Joint answer given by Mrs Fischer Boel on behalf of the Commission Written questions : E-0124/09 , E-0125/09 , E-0126/09 , E-0127/09 , E-0164/09 (19 March 2009) The application to register Kiełbasa Lisiecka as a Protected Geographical Indication was lodged with the Commission on 8 March 2006, as the Honourable Member has indicated. The scrutiny carried out by the Commission concluded that additional information was needed in order to determine whether the application fulfils the requirements of Regulation (EC) No 510/2006 OJ L 93, 31.3.2006. of 20 March 2006 on the protection of geographical indications and designations of origin for agricultural products and foodstuffs. This information was requested from the Polish authorities by a letter dated 16 October 2008. Once the requested information has been submitted, the Commission will re-examine the application. If the Commission, as a result of this examination, considers that the request meets the conditions of the regulation mentioned above, it will publish the summary in the Official Journal with a view to beginning the objection procedure. The application to register ‘Fasola “Piękny Jaś” z Doliny Dunajca’ was lodged with the Commission on 10 July 2008. The Commission expects to complete its scrutiny of the application within the 12-month deadline set down in Article 6 of Regulation (EC) No 510/2006 in order to determine whether it fulfils the conditions set out in the regulation. The application to register ‘Redykołka’ was lodged with the Commission on 22 January 2007. The Commission has completed scrutiny of this application and, once translations have been received. The Single document will be published in the Official Journal in order to begin the objection procedure. This should take place within two months. The application to register ‘Śliwka szydłowska’ was lodged with the Commission on 23 July 2007. The Commission completed the scrutiny of this application in July 2008 and informed the Polish authorities that the application does not meet the requirements laid down in Regulation (EC) No 510/2006. The Polish authorities answered in January 2009. The Commission is in the process of examining the answer and based on this examination will decide whether to proceed to publication or rejection. The application to register ‘Chleb prądnicki’ was lodged with the Commission on 23 April 2008. The Commission expects to complete its scrutiny of the application within the 12‑month deadline set down in Article 6 of Regulation (EC) No 510/2006 in order to determine whether it fulfils the conditions set out in the regulation.
