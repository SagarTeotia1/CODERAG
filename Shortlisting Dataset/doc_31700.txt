The Week Ahead 26 November to 2 December 2007 2007-11-23 - 08:27 Plenary session and committee meetings – Brussels Plenary session Future of Europe. Spanish Prime Minister José Luis Rodríguez Zapatero will set out his views on the future of Europe at the plenary session and will also have a meeting with Parliament President Hans-Gert Pöttering (Wednesday). Charter of Fundamental Rights. MEPs will debate and vote on a report giving Parliament's support to the Charter of Fundamental Rights, ahead of the proclamation of the charter in December (Wednesday). Court of Auditors. The President of the Court of Auditors will present the court's annual report. The House will vote on the appointment of seven members of the court. (Thursday) Other topics on the plenary agenda include a co-decision report (first reading) on the control of the acquisition and possession of weapons, the television broadcasting directive (formerly Television without Frontiers), railway interoperability and economic partnership agreements . In committee Fuel. The Environment Committee will vote on a co-decision report (first reading) on the review of the directive on fuel quality. With oil prices and global warming in the news, MEPs are calling for more ambitious targets (Tuesday). A green EP. At the same committee, President Pöttering and Secretary-General Harald Rømer will sign on behalf of the EP the Eco-Management and Audit Scheme (EMAS) charter, which seeks to promote good environmental practice (Tuesday). Human rights in China. The Subcommittee on Human Rights is holding a hearing on human rights in China in the run-up to the 2008 Olympics (Monday). Terrorism. The Civil Liberties Committee votes on a resolution on the fight against terrorism (Thursday). The committee is also hosting a meeting on justice and home affairs issues with national parliaments (Monday and Tuesday). Council of Europe. Also this week, President Pöttering and his counterpart from the Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe, René van der Linden, will sign a cooperation agreement between the EP and CoE (Wednesday, followed by a press point). 2007-11-26 Press conferences 11:00 Briefing on the parliamentary agenda 11:00 11:30 Brussels, PHS 0A50 Parliamentary committees CONT Committee on Budgetary Control Discussion with the Supervisory Committee of the European Anti-Fraud Office 15:00 18:30 Brussels, ASP 1G3 ENVI Committee on the Environment, Public Health and Food Safety Discussion with Thomas LÖNNGREN, Executive Director, EMEA, on the work of the agency in 2007 and on the work programme for 2008 in the presence of the EP representatives on the Management Board (15:15-16:15) 15:00 18:30 Brussels, PHS 3C50 IMCO Committee on the Internal Market and Consumer Protection Product safety - Discussion with Meglena Kuneva, Commissioner for Health and Consumer Protection (16:30-17:00) Presentation on the Implementation of the Services Directive and of the IMI-System - Discussion with the Commission 15:00 18:30 Brussels, ASP 3E2 DROI Subcommittee on Human Rights Presentation of the EU Annual Report on Human Rights 2007 by the Portuguese Presidency 15:00 19:00 Brussels, PHS 1A2 Delegations Delegation for relations with the Maghreb countries and the Arab Maghreb Union (including Libya) This meeting will take place in the presence of H.E.
