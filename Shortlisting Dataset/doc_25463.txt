Question for written answer P-009149/2011 to the Commission Rule 117 Proinsias De Rossa (S&D) (12 October 2011) Subject: EU‑Morocco Fisheries Partnership Agreement International law requires that economic activities related to the natural resources of a Non‑Self‑Governing Territory, such as Western Sahara, are carried out for the benefit of the people of such Territory, and in accordance with their wishes. What conclusions has the Commission reached regarding the benefit of the EU‑Morocco Fisheries Partnership Agreement (FPA) to the people of Western Sahara? What evidence has the Commission used to draw those conclusions? Has the Joint Committee established in the FPA adequately fulfilled its role in protecting the rights of the people of Western Sahara? Indeed, has the current FPA adequately vindicated the rights of the people of Western Sahara? What strengthened provisions are planned for the proposed FPA to begin in 2012? Is it foreseen to make explicit reference to the rights under international law of the people of Western Sahara to benefit from any economic activities relating to the natural resources of the Territory? Is it foreseen to prohibit EU flagged vessels from the exploitation of the waters of Western Sahara under this agreement?
