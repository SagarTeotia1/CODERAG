Answer given by Mrs Ferrero-Waldner on behalf of the Commission (29 August 2005) 1. The European Commission finances non-governmental organisations (NGO) and non-state actors’ (NSAs) Communication on participation of non-state actors in EC development policy, COM(2002)598 final. ‘The term NSA is used to describe a range of organisations that bring together the principal, existing or emerging, structures of the society outside the government and public administration. NSAs are created voluntarily by citizens, their aim being to promote an issue or an interest, either general or specific. They are independent of the state and can be profit or non-profit-making organisations. The following are examples of NSAs: non-governmental organisations/community based organisations (NGO/CBO) and their representative platforms in different sectors, social partners (trade unions, employers associations), private sector associations and business organisations, associations of churches and confessional movements, universities, cultural associations, media. NSAs as profit/non-profit-making — NSAs as defined above have an important role to play in the development process. This communication deals with support to build the capacities of NSAs in terms of their internal structure and organisation, constituency building through broadening networks, development of leadership qualities and analytical and advocacy skills, sustainable fund raising mechanisms, etc. The NSAs are non-profit-making organisations in this framework. The business sector, acting as profit-making, is covered by this communication only with regard to its participation in the development dialogue and policy implementation. Financial support to the business sector acting as profit-making is provided through different ad hoc instruments.’ actions through specific actions and programmes as follows (see Annex 1 which is sent direct to the Honourable Member and to Parliament’s Secretariat): — Culture of peace: no support for this specific area, but a similar programme covering the resolution/prevention of conflicts and the maintenance of peace issue is available EIDHR — European Initiative for Democracy and Human Rights http://europa.eu.int/comm/europeaid/projects/eidhr/index_en.htm — Long distance adoption: no support. — Homes for single mothers: no support for this specific area, but a similar programme covering ‘violence against women’ issue is available: Daphne II — EU programme to combat violence against children, young people and women: http://europa.eu.int/comm/justice_home/funding/daphne/funding_daphne_en.htm — Another possibility is the gender equality programme aimed to support the activities of organisations active at European level in the field of equality between men and women: http://europa.eu.int/comm/employment_social/equal/index_en.cfm Furthermore, there is a programme of aid for policies and actions on reproductive and sexual health and rights in developing countries dealing with single mothers: http://europa.eu.int/comm/europeaid/cgi/frame12.pl — Humanitarian assistance in conflict and natural disasters situations: the Humanitarian Aid Office (ECHO) is the service of the Commission responsible for humanitarian aid.
