Question for written answer E-012109/2011 to the Commission Rule 117 Zigmantas Balčytis (S&D) (20 December 2011) Subject: Innovative management of hazardous substances in the Baltic Sea region Hazardous substances can be found in all parts of the Baltic Sea. They pose a threat to the environment of the Baltic Sea region and to human health and are affecting ecosystems. If the Baltic Sea region is to be protected, the main task must be to reduce emissions of all kinds, monitor and predict changes in the ecological state of the Baltic Sea and protect that ecological state. An innovative approach is essential in order to curb the risks linked to the presence of chemicals in the water and on the seabed. One of the main projects under the first pillar of the strategy for the Baltic Sea region concerns the ‘innovative management of hazardous substances in the Baltic Sea region’. On the basis of a cost-benefit analysis of the measures in question, what benefits can be expected from the use of innovative measures to deal with hazardous substances? What innovative solutions to the problem of the management of hazardous chemicals are currently being employed? Which countries are involved in implementing this project? What funding is needed to implement this project? What funding is currently being allocated to this project?
