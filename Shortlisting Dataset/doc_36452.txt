16.2.2005 MOTION FOR A RESOLUTION to wind up the debate on the statement by the Commission pursuant to Rule 103(2) of the Rules of Procedure by Martin Schulz and Hannes Swoboda on behalf of the PSE Group on the Commission's 2005 work programme European Parliament resolution on the Commission's 2005 work programme The European Parliament , – having regard to the Commission's 2005 work programme, – having regard to the presentation of that programme by the Commission on 26 January 2005 and the ensuing debate in the presence of the Council, – having regard to its resolution of 16 December 2004 on the Commission's strategic guidelines, – having regard to the current framework agreement governing relations between the European Parliament and the Commission, which was approved by the Conference of Presidents on 29 June 2000, – having regard to Rule 103(2) of its Rules of Procedure, A. whereas the annual work programme is an interinstitutional instrument essential to the effectiveness, transparency and coordination of the European Union's activities, B. whereas the transparency of the legislative process from the preparatory stage onwards, the implementation of the provisions on comitology and guarantees regarding the prompt transposition of Community legislation in the Member States are fundamental to better lawmaking, C. whereas the proposed measures must be accompanied by the appropriations needed to implement them, D. whereas it is essential that the Commission should be in a position to serve the general interest in the European Union, and pointing out that, in that context, independence, the rejection of national prejudices, impartiality, full respect for the EU's values and objectives and the absence of any conflicts of interest are factors vital to securing the confidence of European citizens, E. whereas the Commission's proposals should constitute a road map which enables Parliament to monitor, assess and give its views on the results achieved, 1. Notes the Commission's legislative and work programme for 2005 and the objectives and political priorities laid down therein; regards the programme as unambitious when set against the economic, social and environmental challenges of the next few decades; regrets the general nature of the proposals contained in the programme, in particular those dealing with the social dimension and cohesion policy; 2. Expresses concern at the imbalances between the various political approaches proposed and, in particular, the often excessive liberalism which characterises many of the proposals; 3. Regrets, in particular, the lack of any formal set of practical proposals dealing, in particular, with social policy, the promotion of equal opportunities for men and women, consumer protection policy, measures designed to reduce the disparities in economic development between regions, and cultural issues, in particular media concentration in the European Union; 4. Calls on the Commission to ensure that its proposed communication strategy includes information for citizens about the importance of the Constitution for the future of the European Union and their new rights under the Constitution, including the European citizens’ initiative; Economic growth and job creation 5.
