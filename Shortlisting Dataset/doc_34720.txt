Answer given by Mr Byrne on behalf of the Commission (16 March 2004) Article 6 of Regulation (EC) No 1774/2002 of the Parliament and of the Council of 3 October 2002 laying down health rules concerning animal by-products not intended for human consumption OJ L 273, 10.10.2002 . requires that any protein, except some such as milk or colostrum, is heat treated before being given to farm animals. Furthermore, Article 22 of the same Regulation prohibits intra-species recycling. In accordance with this prohibition, the feeding of processed animal proteins derived from one species to animals of the same species, including the case of pigs, is banned. However, without prejudice of the above mentioned Regulation, enzymes and micro-organisms may be authorized in accordance with Council Directive 70/524/EEC of 23 November 1970 concerning additives in feedstuffs OJ L 270, 14.12.1970 . . The purpose of these rules is to minimize the risk to public and animal health from material derived from animals.
