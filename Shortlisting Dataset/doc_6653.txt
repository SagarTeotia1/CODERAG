Considers that the Moldovan government has not been able to stabilise the economy and establish more democracy, while noting that the Transdniestrian conflict has contributed for a large part to the instability of the whole country; 3. Welcomes that the Moldovan civil society is playing a role in the demilitarisation, decriminalisation and democratisation of the whole Moldovan republic; 4. Supports the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Moldova as the basis for any peaceful solution of the conflict; 5. Welcomes the establishment of a joint constitutional commission, but is disappointed that the Commission has not made any substantial progress; 6. Considers that the peace plan presented by Russia is legitimising the present situation, which has an influence on the development of democracy in Moldova and contributes to the destabilisation of the whole region and awaits with interest a possible peace plan from the Moldovan government; 7. Deplores that the Russian government did not withdraw its arms and troops before the end of 2003; 8. Welcomes the position of most of the ministers taking part in the 11th OSCE ministerial council to provide an OSCE mandate for a multinational peace consolidation mission and unarmed observers, and would like the EU to join this mission and provide observes as well; Urges the governments of Romania, the Ukraine and Russia to refrain from any unilateral interference in Moldova's internal affairs, but to work together with the OSCE, the leading force in the democratisation process in Moldova, the EU and the Council of Europe in order to establish a stable and peaceful development of all countries in the region; Instructs its President to forward this resolution to the Council and Commission, the government of Moldova, the government of Romania, the government of the Ukraine, the government of Russia, the Secretary General of the OSCE and the Secretary General of the Council of Europe.
