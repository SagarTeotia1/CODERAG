WRITTEN QUESTION E-0171/05 by Stefano Zappalà (PPE-DE) and Antonio Tajani (PPE-DE) to the Council (24 January 2005) Subject: Situation in Palestine In view of the fact that: a) presidential elections were held in Palestine on 9 January 2005; b) the European Union was present at that event in the form of a Parliament and Commission delegation; c) the European Union had invested several million euros in the event; d) the elections proceeded in a proper, democratic and transparent fashion; e) the social situation in the territories nonetheless leaves a great deal to be desired; f) the European Union invests a great deal of money in Palestine in order to assist cases in which there is a particular need, would the Council answer the following questions? 1. What kind of - and how much - financial assistance has been made available to the area in question over the last five years? For what purpose and to whom was it paid? 2. Was the aid targeted? If so, who monitored the use to which it was put, and when did such monitoring take place? 3. What aid and/or investment programmes are planned for the next five years? 4. Does the Council not consider that it should adopt a specific action programme for the purpose of providing and/or modernising infrastructure (roads, sewers, aqueducts and railway lines) and public buildings (schools, hospitals, a parliament and community facilities)? 5. Does the Council not consider that it should adopt a specific action programme for the purpose of providing social and cultural centres, health care, domicilary care and whatever else is required in order to raise the local residents’ standard of living to at least an acceptable level?
