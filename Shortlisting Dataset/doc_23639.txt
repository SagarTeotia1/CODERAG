Answer given by Ms Ferrero-Waldner on behalf of the Commission (3 September 2008) Since February 2008, European assistance to the occupied Palestinian territory is channelled through the Pegase mechanism. Pegase Mécanisme Palestino-Européen de Gestion de l'Aide Socio-Economique. , as for the Temporary International Mechanism (TIM), maintains the highest standards of verification and control over the use of EC and donor funds. Advanced monitoring, control and audit systems are set up for all of Pegase activities. These systems ensure the efficient and effective provision of support to Palestinians, while protecting the interests of donors participating in Pegase by ensuring that funds are disbursed with full transparency and accountability and in compliance with the relevant agreements between Pegase and the Palestinian Authority. Payments are executed applying the highest fiduciary control standards and a sophisticated and uniform financial reporting system is being implemented, giving detailed information on all operations processed. Individual beneficiaries are uniformly checked against international sanctions lists. Depending on the type of project and programme financed, on the characteristics of the sector concerned, on the geographical location, on the payment system used and on donors requests, specific ex-ante , real-time and/or ex-post monitoring and control procedures have been put in place. Financial experts and qualified auditors are working with Pegase; they are also complemented by EC and Member States specialists and international and local experts.
