12 March 2004 MOTION FOR A RESOLUTION pursuant to Rule 48 of the Rules of Procedure by Franz Turchi on recognition of obesity as a social inconvenience and as an objective, serious and disabling condition European Parliament resolution on recognition of obesity as a social inconvenience and as an objective, serious and disabling condition The European Parliament , â€“ having regard to Rule 48 of its Rules of Procedure, A. whereas the WHO has declared obesity a 'pan-European epidemic', with half of all Europeans overweight, 25% considered obese and 7% seriously obese - i.e. weighing 60% more than their ideal bodyweight, B. whereas obesity is considered to be the main cause of preventable death from metabolic, cardiovascular and cancer diseases, with very high social and economic costs, C. whereas obese persons can only avail themselves of clinical diagnosis tools with extreme difficulty, 1. Calls on the Commission to recognise serious obesity as an objective disabling condition and in general as a source of social inconvenience; 2. Calls on the Commission to promote and implement measures aimed at managing and curbing the phenomenon of obesity, with resulting beneficial effects in terms of the psycho-physical health of those concerned and the social costs.
