Press enquiries: Marjory van den Broeke (Strasbourg) tel.(33-3) 881 72208 (Brussels) tel.(32-2) 28 44304 e-mail : cont-press@europarl.eu.int Eurostat - no one has taken political responsibility Motion for a resolution further to the Commission statement pursuant to Rule 37(2) of the Rules of Procedure by Jens-Peter Bonde on behalf of the EDD Group on Eurostat Doc.: B5-0218/2004 , B5-0219/2004 , B5-0220/2004 , B5-0222/2004 , B5-0223/2004 , B5-0225/2004 Vote: 22.04.2004 Vote The European Commission has failed to draw the appropriate conclusions from the Eurostat affair and has failed to accept its political responsibility for the matter, either collectively or individually, Parliament said in a resolution adopted by 271 votes in favour, 194 against and 7 abstentions. MEPs demanded that the Commission make a full evaluation over the next three months of the Eurostat affair including all reports by the fraud-fighting office OLAF. MEPs repeated their criticism over the slow response of Commissioners to the warning signs and the poor flow of information within the Commission. Reports about the Eurostat affair so far have revealed that there was no effective political response to public announcements by OLAF about investigations launched into Eurostat, neither by the Commissioner responsible nor the Commissioner responsible for budgetary control; no-one had any oversight of all the pieces of evidence as these slowly gathered; the secretary-general did not pass on any of the information from OLAF; and the Commissioners' private offices ignored or dismissed information that did reach them. MEPs repeated the recommendations made in earlier reports and said that Commissioners are responsible for the wrongdoings of officials in their departments.
