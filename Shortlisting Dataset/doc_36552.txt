Answer given by Mr Borg on behalf of the Commission (22 June 2007) According to the Council Regulation (EC) No 1568/2005 Council Regulation (EC) No 1568/2005 of 20 September 2005 amending Regulation (EC) No 850/98 as regards the protection of deep-water coral reefs from the effects of fishing in certain areas of the Atlantic Ocean, OJ L 252, 28.9.2005. , trawls and other similar methods of fishing are prohibited from being used on vessels operating in the specific area of the Canaries. Compliance with this regulation must be ensured by Member States through appropriate inspections, checks and both administrative and criminal proceedings, as laid down in Regulation (EC) No 2847/93 Council Regulation (EEC) No 2847/93 of 12 October 1993 establishing a control system applicable to the common fisheries policy, OJ L 261, 20.10.1993. establishing a control system applicable to the Common Fisheries policy. The Commission checks and evaluates whether Member States comply with their control obligations by carrying out its own inspections. If necessary, it takes appropriate measures against Member States, such as for example launching infringement procedures in accordance with Article 266 of the EU Treaty. The Commission will check with the Member State concerned whether a violation of the provisions of Regulation (EC) 1568/2005 has occurred in the present instance and what measures the Member State has taken to ensure compliance with, and enforcement of the regulation mentioned. The Commission will then decide whether further action is warranted.
