The terrorist threat, he said, was still hanging over Europe and this was an important matter. "The summer has also seen more terrorism in Iraq, a series of natural disasters and accidents which had brought pain to many, in particular at present in the United States." Mr Borrell had expressed Parliament's solidarity to the relevant authorities. "It is good for us to give some thought to the pain of families of victims of accidents and conflicts. We should think of the USA, where there is a drama of incomparable size, with many killed, thousands displaced and great structural damage." he said. Terrorism, he said was killing many people in Iraq every day, and a rumour of an attack had led to the death of more than a thousand people when panic struck a crowded bridge. More than 400 people had lost their lives in air accidents over the summer, he continued, with plane crashes in Indonesia, Greece, Venezuela, Peru and the Baltic, as well as a helicopter crash killing Spanish soldiers in Afghanistan. The summer months had also seen dramatic flooding in Austria, Romania, Bulgaria, Germany and Switzerland, killing dozens and displacing many others. At the same time, there had been economic damage caused by drought in other parts of Europe, and still more deaths as a result of major forest fires in Portugal, France and Spain. "Faced with these dramas, the EU was quick to offer support to those affected, he said. As an expression of solidarity, condolences and sympathy to the families of all the victims, MEPs then rose to their feet to observe a minute's silence.
