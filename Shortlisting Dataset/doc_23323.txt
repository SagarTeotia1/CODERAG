These cooperation mechanisms foresee regular meetings between European and Chinese product safety experts (including regulators, standardisers, industry and EU Member State enforcement authorities), a continuous exchange of information on applicable safety requirements and related standards, the organisation of targeted activities for manufacturers in China and importers or distributors in the EU as well as training of the Chinese authority's personnel to improve enforcement activities in China. The cooperation with the Chinese authorities enables the Commission to monitor the effectiveness of their actions and ensure that EU safety requirements are being adhered to. In addition improvements are expected in the near future as a result of the entry into force, on 1 January 2010, of Regulation (EC) No 765/2008 OJ L 218 of 13.8.2008. , which obliges Member States to perform controls at external borders on safety aspects of products coming from third countries.
