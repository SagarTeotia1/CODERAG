Calls on the Israeli Government immediately to put an end to all acts that constitute serious breaches of international law and lead to a worsening and escalation of the situation and intolerable violence against the civilian population; 13. Endorses the call for a mutual and simultaneous ceasefire by the Israeli army and Palestinian armed groups; 14. Calls on the European Union to take strong initiatives to help break the vicious circle of violence and the military escalation and to relaunch the peace process; calls for an international observation and protection mission to be sent under the aegis of the United Nations as a step towards a de-escalation in the violence; 15. Condemns the Israeli Government for building the separation wall, which constitutes a de facto annexation of the Palestinian territories by Israel and is illegal under international law; calls for it to be immediately dismantled and for the recent ruling of the Hague International Court to be complied with; 16. Reiterates its support for the 'Geneva Accord' and for all those involved on a daily basis in work for peace and reconciliation between the peoples of the region, in particular those Israeli reservists refusing to serve in the Occupied Territories; 17. Instructs its President to forward this resolution to the Council, the Commission, the Israeli Government and Parliament, the Palestinian National Authority and Legislative Council, the United Nations Secretary-General, the Russian Federal Government and Parliament, the US Presidency and Congress.
