Question for written answer E-9837/2010 to the Commission Rule 117 Rodi Kratsa-Tsagaropoulou (PPE) (26 November 2010) Subject: Europeana library and digital technology in the European Union Digitalisation at European level is one of the flagship initiatives under the Europe 2020 strategy for emerging from the crisis and preparing the EU economy for the challenges of the coming decade. In this connection, the Europeana digital library was opened to the public on 20 November 2008, providing links to date with 6 million digital records containing information about Europe’s rich heritage, with the aim of making the more user-friendly European sources of information available online. Measures are being planned to improve the Europeana.eu website. The Commission has indicated that the Europeana Version 1.0 is being developed and will be ready in 2010, providing links to over 10 million digital items. In view of this: 1. What is the Commission’s assessment of progress by the Member States in creating and implementing projects in the field of digital technology? 2. What is Europe's relationship with the American giants in this field and to what extent is it dependent on them? 3. Are the Europeana digital library and the measures to improve it based on European or American technology? What is the extent and substance of Member State contributions and what role is being played by the Commission with regard to this project?
