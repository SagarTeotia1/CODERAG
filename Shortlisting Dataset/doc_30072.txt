Question for written answer E-000738/2011 to the Commission Rule 117 Martin Ehrenhauser (NI) (10 February 2011) Subject: Data retention Germany's Justice Minister Sabine Leutheusser-Schnarrenberger has put forward a proposal for a data retention implementing law. According to a report by Zeit Online of 18 January 2011, EU Commissioner Viviane Reding supports this proposal, describing it as a ‘promising approach’. 1. The solution proposed by the German Justice Minister continues to provide for data to be retained without any specific reason and irrespective of grounds for suspicion. To what extent can data retention without any specific reason be justified? 2. To what extent are Internet users being placed under a general suspicion? 3. The Court of Justice of the European Communities and the European Court of Human Rights have described the comprehensive recording or collection of personal data as disproportionate. How can the proposal nonetheless be described as a ‘promising approach’? 4. The mass recording of citizens' communication activities continues to contravene the EU Charter of Fundamental Rights. How can the proposal nonetheless be described as a ‘promising approach’? 5. How does the Commission stand vis-à-vis the Council of Europe's Convention on Cybercrime, in particular the provisions on the expeditious preservation and targeted collection of traffic data as an alternative to data retention?
