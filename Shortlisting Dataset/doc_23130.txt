Answer given by Mr Piebalgs on behalf of the Commission (25 February 2008) 1. The Commission is aware of the Epidemiological Study on Childhood Cancer in the Vicinity of Nuclear Power Plants, carried out by the German Childhood Cancer Register. The Commission is going to consult the Group of Experts referred to in Article 31 of the Euratom Treaty, at the meeting of 25 to 27 November 2008, and its Working Party on Research Implications on Health and Safety Standards, in order to summarise current knowledge of childhood cancer and leukaemia in the vicinity of nuclear power stations, and to identify, if necessary, potential policy and/or regulatory implications. 2. The Commission is aware of various epidemiological studies on childhood cancer, carried out in other countries. The results of these studies will also be considered in the abovementioned analysis by the Group of Experts referred to in Article 31 of the Euratom Treaty and its Working Party on Research Implications on Health and Safety Standards. 3. Based on its current state of knowledge, the Commission believes that workers in, and the public around nuclear power plants in Member States are appropriately protected. 4. The Commission is prepared to inform the Parliament and the Council about the results of the abovementioned consultation of the Group of Experts referred to in Article 31 of the Euratom Treaty, and of any specific conclusion drawn by the World Health Organisation (WHO) on the same issue.
