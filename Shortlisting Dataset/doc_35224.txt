Answer given by Ms Kroes on behalf of the Commission (8 April 2011) The Commission has set out its strategy for broadband in the Digital Agenda for Europe A Digital Agenda for Europe: COM(2010)245. and, in addition, gave guidance in the implementation of this strategy in the broadband communication European broadband: investing in digitally driven growth: COM(2010)472. . These documents stress the importance of wireless broadband (terrestrial and satellite) in ensuring coverage of all areas particularly in remote and rural regions. The Digital Agenda also highlighted the need to ‘coordinate the technical and regulatory conditions applying to spectrum use and, where necessary, harmonise spectrum bands to create economies of scale in equipment markets and allow consumers to use the same equipment and avail themselves of the same services across the EU’ A Digital Agenda for Europe, op. cit., Section 2.1.4, page 14. . In this context, the Commission adopted a proposal for a radio spectrum policy programme Proposal for a decision of the European Parliament and of the Council establishing the first radio spectrum policy programme: COM(2010)471. which aims at delivering on the broadband targets of the Digital Agenda.
