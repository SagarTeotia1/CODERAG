Answer given by Mr Ciolo≈ü on behalf of the Commission (14 March 2012) Where the Commission entrusts the Member States with the implementation of certain measures, the Member State has primary responsibility for setting up a management and control system, either on a national or regional level, which complies with the requirements of the relevant Regulations. In the framework of the clearance of accounts procedure, the Commission verifies the effective functioning of this system and makes financial corrections where necessary. As far as the market situation for peaches of the 2011 harvest is concerned, prices in Spain were indeed exceptionally low during certain periods of time. Against this background, the price quoted by the Honourable Member of the European Parliament reflects this situation and there are no elements to conclude that the withdrawal mechanism has been misused.
