9/05/2005 MOTION FOR A RESOLUTION with request for inclusion in the agenda for the debate on cases of breaches of human rights, democracy and the rule of law pursuant to Rule 115 of the Rules of Procedure by Johan van Hecke on behalf of the ALDE Group on the situation of human rights in Myanmar European Parliament resolution on the situation of human rights in Myanmar The European Parliament , - recalling its previous resolutions on the subject, - having regard to its report on the 2004 human rights situation adopted on 28 April 2005, - having regard to the Council's Common Position of the General Affairs Council of 25 April 2005, extending restrictive measures against Burma/Myanmar for one year and amending Common Position 2004/423/CFSP of April 2004, - having regard to the Charter of the United Nations, the Universal Declaration of Human Rights and the other human rights international instruments, - recalling that Myanmar is a party to the Convention on the Rights of the Child, the Convention on the Elimination of all forms of Discrimination against women, the Convention on the Prevention and Punishment of the Crime of Genocide, the Geneva Conventions of 12 August 1949 on the Protection of Victims of War, as well as the Convention concerning Forced or Compulsory Labour, 1930 and the Convention of the Right to organise, 1948, of the International Labour Organisation, - having regard to the resolution adopted during the 61st session of the UN Commission for Human Rights in Geneva, A. reaffirming that the will of the people is the basis of the authority of government and that the will of the people of Myanmar was clearly expressed in the elections held in 1990, B. recalling that the establishment of a genuine democratic government in Myanmar is essential for the realisation of all human rights and fundamental freedoms, C. taking note of the reconvening of the National Convention on 17 February 2005, without the participation of the democratic opposition, and the suspension of the Convention on 31 March, while recognising that the government of Myanmar has announced that it would reconvene at the end of 2005, D. deeply concerned by the fact that, according to international NGO reports such as Christian Solidarity Worldwide, the Burmese army has used chemical weapons against the Karenni people of Burma, 1. condemns the extension of the house arrest of National League for Democracy General Secretary, Aung San Suu Kyi and her deputy, Tin Oo, for another year and the persistent denial of their human rights and fundamental freedoms, including freedom of movement and association, as well as the continued detention of other senior leaders of the National League for Democracy and of the leadership of other political parties or ethnic minorities, particularly the recent detention of Khun Htun Oo and Sao Nyunt Lwin, Chairman and General Secretary respectively of the Shan Nationalities League for Democracy, and Sao Hso Ten, Chairman of the Shan State Peace Council, 2. condemns the continuing restrictions placed on the National League for Democracy and other political parties which prevented them from
