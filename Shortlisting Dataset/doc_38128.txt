Calls on all sides in Sri Lanka to demonstrate their commitment to peace by reinforcing the ceasefire, which is essential for any progress, and working together towards a peaceful settlement of the ethnic issue that meets the legitimate aspirations of all communities in Sri Lanka; 4. Calls on the Government of Sri Lanka to ratify the ICC Treaty as a deterrent against war crimes and crimes against humanity; 5. Congratulates the people of Sri Lanka for the presidential elections in November 2005, which were conducted peacefully and smoothly in the south, but condemns the actions - including an enforced boycott, taken by the LTTE - in some parts of the north and east which prevented people from exercising their democratic right to vote; 6. Condemns the continuing use of violence by all sides, particularly since November 2005; urges the LTTE to renounce violence and the Government to ensure that paramilitary groups stop their violent actions which contribute to deteriorating the situation; 7.
