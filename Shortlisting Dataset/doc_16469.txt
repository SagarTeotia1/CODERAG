On 15 November in Washington DC, the G-20 will hold a key summit on resolving the financial crisis and modernising the world's financial institutions. MEPs will vote on a resolution on the Commission work programme. What is the G-20? The members of the G-20 are the finance ministers and central bank governors of 19 countries, country leaders will attend the summit in Washington DC: Argentina, Australia, Brazil, Canada, China, France, Germany, India, Indonesia, Italy, Japan, Mexico, Russia, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, South Korea, Turkey, the United Kingdom and the United States of America. The European Union is also a member, represented by the rotating Council presidency and the European Central Bank. To ensure global economic fora and institutions work together, the Managing Director of the International Monetary Fund (IMF) and the President of the World Bank, plus the chairs of the International Monetary and Financial Committee and Development Committee of the IMF and World Bank, also participate in G-20 meetings on an ex-officio basis.
