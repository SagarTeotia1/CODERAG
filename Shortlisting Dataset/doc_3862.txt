Question for written answer E-005538/2011 to the Commission Rule 117 Proinsias De Rossa (S&D) (9 June 2011) Subject: Human rights abuses in Bahrain On 9 April 2011, Bahraini human rights defender Abdulhadi Al-Khawaja, a former Human Rights Protection Coordinator for Frontline and a former President of the Bahrain Centre for Human Rights (BCHR), was arrested and beaten unconscious by police in Manama, Bahrain. His current whereabouts, along with those of two of his sons-in-law, Wafi Almajid and Hussein Ahmed, remain unknown, and they remain in detention and are believed to be at high risk of torture. What initiatives is the Commission taking to ensure that these men are released and that their human rights are respected?
