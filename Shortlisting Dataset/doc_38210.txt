Question for written answer E-008437/2011 to the Commission Rule 117 Ramon Tremosa i Balcells (ALDE) (21 September 2011) Subject: Peñalba-Isona high voltage power line project In his answer to Question P‑002257/2011 of 7 April 2011, Commissioner Potočnik, on behalf of the Commission, said that: ‘In order to know the details of the case and to investigate the issues raised in the Honourable Member’s question, the Commission will request information from the competent Spanish authorities concerning compliance with the relevant requirements under EU environmental law. In particular, the Commission will ask the Spanish authorities how they have applied the provisions of the EIA Directive’. In light of this statement and the fact that five months have passed since the Commission’s answer: 1. Can the Commission confirm whether it has received an answer and the necessary and appropriate clarifications from the Spanish Government about ‘the Peñalba-Isona high voltage power line’ project? If so, what clarifications were made? 2. Is the Commission satisfied with the government’s explanations? Are they adequate to ensure compliance with Directive 85/337/EEC Directive 85/337/EEC (OJ L 175, 5.7.1985) as amended by Directive 97/11/EC (OJ L 73, 14.3.1997), Directive 2003/35/EC (OJ L 156, 25.6.2003) and Directive 2009/31/EC (OJ L 140, 5.6.2009). ? 3. If the Spanish Government has not yet responded, when is the deadline set by the Commission?
