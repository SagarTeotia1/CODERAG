Question for written answer E-5129/2010 to the Commission Rule 117 Ivailo Kalfin (S&D) (7 July 2010) Subject: European territorial cooperation between Bulgaria and Greece The Bulgaria–Greece European territorial cooperation programme for the programming period 2007‑13 is a regional programme designed to stimulate cross‑border cooperation. The programme covers joint projects with Greece and focuses on strengthening cooperation with a view to fostering balanced and sustainable economic and social development across European as a whole. European territorial cooperation is helping these regions to overcome the specific problems arising from their relative isolation from the national economy and to promote the creation and development of networks for cooperation between the two sides of the border and the establishing of links between these structures and broader EU structures. The European territorial cooperation programme is of exceptional importance as it not only provides assistance to the regions targeted but also stimulates the development of the country as a whole. 1. How, in the Commission’s view, is the development of cross‑border cooperation between Bulgaria and Greece progressing? 2. What are the reasons for the very lengthy delays in the approval of projects?
