Answer given by Mr Oettinger on behalf of the Commission (26 October 2011) According to the information available to the Commission, 20 Member States (AT, BE, CY, CZ, DE, DK, ES, FI, FR, HU, IE, IT, LU, NL, PL, PT, SE, SI, SK, UK) have introduced (or plan to introduce) some type of sustainability criteria for solid biomass used in electricity and heating. Only two Member States (UK, BE) have implemented — more or less in full — the recommendations contained in the 2010 Commission communication on sustainability of solid and gaseous biomass http://eur-lex.europa.eu/Notice.do?val=509099:cs&lang=en&list=509099:cs,&pos=1&page=1&nbl=1&pgs=10&hwords= . Most of the sustainability regulations introduced by the abovementioned Member States are binding.
