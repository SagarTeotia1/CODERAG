WRITTEN QUESTION P-1898/10 by Monica Luisa Macovei (PPE) to the Commission (22 March 2010) Subject: Combating corruption through the Stockholm Programme — whereas 78 % of European Union citizens believe that corruption is a major concern in their country (Eurobarometer, 2009), — concerned by the significant costs generated by corruption and its negative impact upon the financial stability of the Union, as proved recently in Greece where generalised corruption in the public sector was acknowledged by its Prime Minister, — having regard to the ‘Stockholm Programme — An open and secure Europe serving and protecting the citizens [for 2010‑14]’, as adopted by the Council (17024/09, 2 December 2009), Section 4.4.5 on Economic crime and corruption, particularly the reference to the development of indicators to measure anti-corruption efforts in the Member States and of a comprehensive anti-corruption policy, — having regard to the European Parliament resolution on the Stockholm Programme ( P7_TA(2009)0090 ), particularly paragraph 127 on promoting a more robust fight against corruption based on an objectives-oriented plan and on a periodic evaluation of the anti-corruption measures taken by the Member States, — aware of the urgent need to act to combat corruption in the Member States to protect the taxpayers' money and the Union's financial stability, 1. What is the current stage of the process (i) establishing an effective and clear mechanism for measuring the anti-corruption measures in the Member States, and (ii) developing the anti-corruption policy? 2. What steps has the Commission planned to reach the objectives referred to in question 1 above? 3. What timetable does the Commission envisage for reaching the objectives referred to in question 1 above?
