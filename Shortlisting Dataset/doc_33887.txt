The President informed the House that he had been present in Aachen on 13 May when Polish Prime Minister Donald Tusk had been awarded the Charlemagne Prize. He had also attended the ceremony for the award of the European Charlemagne Youth Prize to a German project on 11 May 2010. The President mentioned the recent general elections in Hungary and the United Kingdom. 3 Approval of the minutes of the previous sitting The minutes of the previous sitting were approved. ° ° ° ° The following spoke: Mario Mauro and Gianni Pittella , who pointed out that two Italian soldiers had been killed that day in an attack near Herat in Afghanistan; Geoffrey Van Orden , who echoed the sentiments expressed by the two previous speakers and went on to highlight the role of British soldiers involved in NATO's mission in Afghanistan; and Jacky Hénin , who mentioned the victims of accidents at work. The President paid tribute to all the European soldiers fighting terrorism and violence in Afghanistan and throughout the world. 4 Calendar of part-sessions for 2011 The Conference of Presidents had presented its proposal for the calendar of part-sessions for 2011.
