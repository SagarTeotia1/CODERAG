For the purposes of applying Article 82 of the EC Treaty, it does not make any difference whether the undertaking concerned is based in the EU or outside the EU if the abuse restricts competition within the common market or in a substantial part of it and as long as it may affect trade between Member States. This allows the Commission to take action against abuses committed by undertakings based outside the EU, as it is clear for example from the Commission's 2004 Decision against Microsoft, which was upheld by the Court in September 2007. Conversely, the Commission does not have jurisdiction over abuses of dominant companies outside the EU in so far as these abuses do not affect competition within the common market or a substantial part of it and do not affect trade between Member States. However, the Commission cooperates extensively with national competition authorities of third countries with a view to them creating and applying a legal framework preventing abuses of dominant position.
