WRITTEN QUESTION E-4226/05 by Proinsias De Rossa (PSE) to the Commission (16 November 2005) Subject: Situation of Roma and travellers in Europe What has been the Commission's response to the EP resolution of 28 April 2005 on the situation of the Roma in the EU (P6_TA(2005)0151), most notably: — paragraph 4, which urged it to include the issue of combating anti-gypsyism/Romaphobia among its priorities for the 2007 European year of equal opportunities for all; — paragraph 5, which urged it to ensure that candidate countries make real efforts to strengthen the rule of law and protect human and minority rights, particularly those of the Roma population; — paragraph 6, which urged it to prepare a Communication on how the EU can best coordinate and promote efforts to improve the situation of the Roma and to adopt an action plan with clear recommendations to Member States and candidate countries; — paragraph 21, which urged it to work with the nine European countries that launched the Decade of Roma Inclusion initiative in February 2005 in order to align relevant EU programme funding to realise this initiative, and; — paragraph 22, which urged it to publicly encourage governments to ensure that when funding programmes are aimed at Roma, Roma actors are fully involved in the design, implementation and monitoring of such projects?
