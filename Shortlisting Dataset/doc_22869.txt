Answer given by Mr Vitorino on behalf of the Commission (4 November 2004) The Commission has noted the initiative of 28 April 2004 of France, Ireland, Sweden and United Kingdom for a Council Framework Decision on the retention of data processed and stored in connection with the provision of publicly available electronic communication services or data on public communication networks for the purpose of prevention, investigation, detection and prosecution of crime and criminal offences, including terrorism. The Commission would respond to the various questions raised by the Honourable Member as follows: 1. Following an ad hoc meeting of the Forum on Organised Crime Prevention on data retention on 14 June 2004, the Commission held a public workshop on data retention on 21 September 2004. This was part of the open consultation process foreseen by the Commission in its Communication of 16 June 2004 on improving access to information for law enforcement purposes COM(2004) 429 final. . The intention was to identify and discuss existing data retention practices, for both business and law enforcement purposes in the Member States, and to address the extent of the need for an EU-wide regime of data retention for law enforcement purposes. 2. and 3. The Commission is currently determining its position on this initiative, including its position on the legal basis, according to the methodology it adopted on 17 January 2001 with regard to initiatives taken by Member States in the context of Title IV of the Treaty establishing the European Community and Title VI of the Treaty on European Union SEC(2001) 70. .
