WRITTEN QUESTION E-2376/05 by Herbert Bösch (PSE) to the Commission (4 July 2005) Subject: Suspected corruption in connection with EADS contract in Romania According to a report by the Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung of 7 June 2005, the Romanian public prosecutor is investigating suspected corruption in connection with a EUR 650 million border security contract in Romania that was awarded without a public call for tenders. The party to the contract with the Romanian State is the German‑French concern EADS. The newspaper reports that EADS is under suspicion of overcharging Romania for services and including services for which PHARE money had already been provided. — In what way is the Community participating in the financing of the EUR 650 million contract? — Did the Commission approve of the award of the contract without a public call for tenders? — What does the Commission know about the allegations referred to in the FAZ, and what steps has it taken to safeguard the Community's financial interests?
