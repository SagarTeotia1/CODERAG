Calls on the Commission and the Member States to ensure that the achievement of the Millennium Development Goals is central to the Union’s external policy and budget and that explicit reference is made to those Goals in all development policy implementing instruments; calls, in that connection, on the EU to provide a sum of € 1 billion, as far as possible in the form of additional funds, to take the lead in the global fight against, for example, Aids and malaria; 37. Calls on the Commission actively to promote, in its policies and all resulting measures, fair trade as a means of reducing poverty and achieving tangible benefits for the developing and least-developed countries in the negotiations in the current Doha Development Round and beyond; stresses that trade liberalisation should not be seen as an objective in itself, but should contribute to the genuine development of the less-advanced nations without stifling their own potential in that area; Governance and regulation in the Union 38. Encourages the Commission to implement its proposals to improve governance and regulation in the Union; acknowledges that regulatory simplification and assessments of the impact of new legislative proposals are important, but takes the view that they must not undermine the acquis communautaire ; 39. Opposes any attempt to exploit the objective of improving the quality of legislation to make de facto changes to legislative procedures or the balance of powers among the Community institutions; ° ° ° 40. Instructs its President to forward this resolution to the Council, the Commission and the governments and parliaments of the Member States.
