European Council EU government leaders’ Summit in Brussels Council and Commission statements on the outcome of the European Council, including the Intergovernmental Conference (Brussels, 16/17 October 2003) Debate: 22.10.2003 Parliament will debate the outcome of the European government leaders' summit held in Brussels on 16 and 17 October. Italian Prime Minister Mr Silvio Berlusconi will be present at the debate. Government leaders of the present and acceding EU Member States meet on 16-17 October 2003 in Brussels, the first summit during the Italian EU Presidency. On the agenda is the Intergovernmental Conference, preparing a new EU Treaty, as well as the usual exchange of views on the main developments at Union and international level. Some of the topics at the summit are economic growth and employment, joint management of the EU's external borders, management of migration flows and judicial cooperation, Trans-European Networks, the "Wider Europe" initiative, the Western Balkans, and the situation in Iraq, the Middle East and Iran. External Relations Urgent call on both Israel and Palestinians not to declare the peace process dead Emilio MENÉNDEZ del VALLE (PES, E) Report on peace and dignity in the Middle East (2002/2166(INI)) Doc.: A5-0351/2003 Procedure : Own-initiative report Debate: 22.10.2003 In tabling a non-binding motion for a resolution on behalf of the Foreign Affairs Committee, Emilio MENÉNDEZ DEL VALLÉ (PES, E) urgently calls on both the Israeli government and the Palestinian Authority on Tuesday not to declare the peace process dead and to commit themselves to the implementation of the "road map" set out for reaching a peace agreement. Should the road map break down in the immediate future, an international mandate in Palestine should be established under the authority of the Quartet (the UN, the EU, the US and Russia), including an international force on the ground. The committee says the Palestinian Authority should clearly and firmly come out in support of the new Palestinian government. This government should continue reorganising the Palestinian security forces, should re-establish public order and should make concrete and visible efforts to dismantle terrorist organisations. It should pursue the reforms it has set in motion and hold free, fair and transparent elections as soon as possible.
