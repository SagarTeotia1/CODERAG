Question for written answer E-003872/2011 to the Commission Rule 117 William (The Earl of) Dartmouth (EFD) (19 April 2011) Subject: Increase in interest rates In the light of the views of economists, for example, Gerard Lyons, chief economist at Standard Chartered Bank, who has warned that the challenge in Europe is that ‘one size’ does not fit all. Higher official borrowing costs would be ‘the wrong policy, for the wrong reason, at the wrong time’ (source: Financial Times , 4 April 2011), or Nouriel Roubini, a professor of Economics at New York University, who has said that ‘My fear is that tightening by the ECB is going to exacerbate the growth, fiscal and financial stresses of the periphery’ (source: Financial Times , 4 April 2011): Does the Commission support the European Central Bank's decision to tighten monetary policy in the euro area by increasing interest rates?
