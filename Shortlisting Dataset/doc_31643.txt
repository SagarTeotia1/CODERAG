WRITTEN QUESTION E-5717/08 by Nils Lundgren (IND/DEM) to the Commission (21 October 2008) Subject: The possibility for the Member States to oppose the import of energy In Title 1 of the Treaty of Lisbon on categories and areas of Union competence, Article 2c states that the Union shall share competence with the Member States in a number of so-called principal areas, one of which is energy. Does the Commission consider that the Treaty of Lisbon gives the individual Member States the sovereign right to decide which type of energy they wish to import?
