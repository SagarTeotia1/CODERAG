2 September 2003 MOTION FOR A RESOLUTION further to the Commission statement pursuant to Rule 37(2) of the Rules of Procedure by Enrique Barón Crespo, Dagmar Roth-Behrendt, Hannes Swoboda, Carlos Lage, Joan Colom i Naval, Guido Sacconi and Gilles Savary on behalf of the PSE Group on the effects of the summer 2003 heat wave European Parliament resolution on the effects of the summer 2003 heat wave The European Parliament, – having regard to the exceptional heat wave and drought which hit Europe and other parts of the world in the course of the last weeks, – having regard to the dramatic consequences of these climatic conditions, namely huge fires causing numerous victims in Portugal, France, Spain, Croatia, Russia and Canada, alarming ozone peaks and a dramatic increase in the death rate especially of the elderly, – having regard to the social and economic effects in the agriculture and forest industries, – having regard to Council Regulation (EC) 2012/2002 of 11 November 2002 establishing the European Union Solidarity Fund, – having regard to Article 87(2) of the EC Treaty, – having regard to the decisions taken by the Commission at its meeting of 27/8/2003, – whereas solidarity between Member States in the case of natural disasters is part of the core of the European project, – whereas the European Parliament called on the Commission to pay special attention to the economic and social structural problems caused by natural disasters and to establish an appropriate instrument to help solve these problems, and whereas Regulation (EC) 2012/2002 consequently established an European Union Solidarity Fund, 1. Expresses its solidarity and deepest sympathy with the populations affected by the summer 2003 heat wave and fires, and with the families of the numerous persons who lost their lives; pays tribute to the dedication of the many rescuers and medical staff whose hard and continuous work helped to limit human and material damage; strongly condemns the criminal acts of pyromania committed in several cases; 2. Welcomes the solidarity shown by the EU Member States with the affected regions and the praiseworthy help provided to their authorities and rescue services; calls for strengthened cooperation in the field of civil protection within the EU; 3. Welcomes the Commission's rapid proposal to grant EUR 31 655 million from the European Solidarity Fund to Portugal, and encourages the Commission to rapidly take the necessary steps to provide other EU countries affected by fires with similar support; 4. Welcomes the adoption by the Commission of regulations intended to put cereals held by various intervention agencies at the disposal of the internal market in order to offset the deficit caused by the drought; 5. Calls on the Commission to rapidly apply Article 87(2) of the EC Treaty with a view to granting appropriate financial aid to all sectors affected by the disaster; 6. Insists on the need for flexibility in the management of the structural funds, in order to allow efficient reprogramming within the national allocations aimed at better tackling new local economic and social problems caused by catastrophes; 7.
