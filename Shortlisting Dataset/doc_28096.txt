Deplores the long-standing harassment of political opponents; 2. Calls for the immediate and unconditional release of Mr Melainine and the two other MPF members; 3. Calls for a re-run of the trial, before a new court and with international observers, in order to guarantee the legitimate right to a fair trial of the three members of the MPF; 4. Calls on the government of Mauritania to respect the Constitution, guarantee the right of free expression and allow the formation and registration of political parties; 5. Urges the government of Mauritania to take the opportunity of the forthcoming elections to demonstrate its scrupulous respect for democratic rules; 6. Considers that the legislative and municipal elections scheduled for October 2001 must be postponed to enable all those who are politically active to stand; calls on the government of Mauritania to guarantee the democratic rights of all opposition parties and representatives and to establish without delay an independent national electoral commission; 7. Calls on the government of Mauritania to agree that the next elections should be held in the presence of international observers, including a delegation of Members of the European Parliament; 8. Calls on the government of Mauritania to take immediate steps to put an end to the continued practice of slavery; 9. Calls on the Council and the Commission to open consultations with Mauritania in accordance with the procedure laid down in Article 96 of the Cotonou Agreement if the government of Mauritania does not provide an adequate and rapid response to the concerns outlined above; 10. Instructs its President to forward this resolution to the Council, the Commission, the government of Mauritania, the Secretary-General of the United Nations and the Co-Presidents of the ACP-EU Parliamentary Assembly.
