WRITTEN QUESTION E-0210/09 by Hannes Swoboda (PSE) to the Council (26 January 2009) Subject: Impact on the citizens of EU Member States of the gas dispute between Russia and Ukraine The main responsibility for these consequences lies with the countries concerned and with the energy companies responsible. In this context the questions to the Council indicated below arise. 1. What steps did the Council take once it became clear that Ukraine had not used the resources provided by the European Union for gas measuring stations in the Ukraine and had not set up any of the promised stations? 2. What steps did the Council take once it became clear that the agreements signed by Ukraine/Naftogas and Russia/Gazprom in October 2008 did not result in the conclusion of actual contracts by the target dates of end October/beginning November 2008? 3. What steps did the Council take in November and December to prepare Europe for a potential interruption in gas supplies as from 1 January 2009? 4. What consequences did the Council announce to the Russians and Ukrainians in the event of inadequate cooperation? 5. What consequences did the Council announce to the Russians and Ukrainians in the event of breaches of contract? 6. What conclusions does the Council draw from the current crisis, so that the public can be spared such crises and, above all, interruptions in gas supplies? 7. Will the Council propose actual measures for joint monitoring of gas in transit, especially in Ukraine, and table proposals for organising transit jointly in future?
