Answer given by Mr Rehn on behalf of the Commission (11 December 2006) As a future Member State of the European Union, Romania will participate in the comprehensive strategy on the rights of the child that was developed by the Commission in order to promote and safeguard childrens' rights. Individual measures are laid down in the communication ‘Towards an EU strategy for the rights of a child’ COM(2006)367 final. . An inter-service Working Group of the Commission on children's rights will evaluate the national and European policies to that effect. Moreover, a European Forum for the Rights of the Child will be created, to bring together the stakeholders in child protection. Romanian child protection experts will, in this context, exchange experiences and best practices with their counterparts from other countries. This will result in a process of permanent monitoring and support for child protection in Romania. Any Member State failing to carry out its responsibility in this area is subject to possible action under the acquis .
