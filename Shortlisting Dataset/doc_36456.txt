Calls for a completely environmentally-friendly approach to transport policy which focuses on a modal shift away from the roads towards the railways, inland waterways or short-haul maritime routes, thereby lending its full backing to Commission programmes such as ‘Marco Polo’; 25. Calls for a policy to prevent maritime pollution and for a continuation of the campaign for safety at sea, involving the establishment of a European Coastguard Service, compulsory piloting in sensitive areas, the fitting-out of ports of refuge and measures to combat flags of convenience; 26. Emphasises that the debate on the future European Fisheries Fund (EFF) should be used as an opportunity to consider more carefully the adjustments required in terms of the renewal and modernisation of the EU fleet, bearing in mind the need for sustainable management of resources and for a viable industry which can provide jobs, prevent social exclusion and supply EU consumers with high-quality products; Area of freedom, security and justice 27. Welcomes the Commission’s proposals to strengthen operational cooperation and the exchange of information and to make travel documents more secure, as part of the fight against crime and terrorism, but emphasises, also, that fundamental safeguards and rights, in particular the right to the protection of personal data, must be respected at all times; calls on the Commission to draw up the long-awaited proposal for a framework decision on data protection under the third pillar; 28. Emphasises the importance of strengthening judicial cooperation on criminal law civil law matters and of guaranteeing access to justice, and calls on the Commission to draw up proposals based on the mutual recognition of decisions and on a minimum degree of ion, in particular in the areas of the assessment and use of evidence, the enforcement of sentences and coercive measures, procedural safeguards and the rights of detainees; welcomes the extension of judicial cooperation to cover certain aspects of family law; 29. Notes the proposals on the opening-up of avenues for legal action and on border checks, but points out that the joint rules governing legal immigration must not be drawn up solely on the basis of the needs of the European labour market, but must also take account of migrants’ needs and must respect their fundamental rights to the letter; calls on the Commission to draw up measures to define the status of migrants and to foster their active integration into society, and calls for minimum standards to govern the award and withdrawal of refugee status and a strengthening of the status of asylum-seekers; Increased Union responsibility on the international stage 30.
