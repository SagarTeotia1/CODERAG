13 September 2004 MOTION FOR A RESOLUTION tabled for the debate on cases of breaches of human rights, democracy and the rule of law pursuant to Rule 115 of the Rules of Procedure by Mrs POLI BORTONE On behalf of the UEN Group On harassment on minorities in Voivodina ( Serbia-Montenegro) European parliament resolution on harassment of minorities in Voivodina (Serbia-Montenegro) The European parliament, - Having regard to the Stabilisation and Association Process in South Eastern Europe and the EU efforts to promote democratisation in Serbia and Montenegro, - Having regard to its previous Resolutions on Serbia Montenegro, A. Whereas while some events against the ethnic minorities can appear to be local and secondary - if they are not tackled in an adequate way- may have serious and widespread consequences, B. Whereas in the Voivodina province of Serbia and Montenegro there is a growing number of cases of intimidation and violence against citizens of Hungarian ethnicity, such as : * violation of Hungarian and Croatian tombs in many towns * a massive occurrence of Anti-Hungarian inscriptions * burning the national flag of the Republic of Hungary * physical aggression by the police against a mayor of Hungarian ethnicity, C Whereas there was no reaction from the Voivodina authorities against such acts, 1 Considers that full respect for the different minorities in the territory of the Balkan States is crucial for the development of all these States taking into account the stabilisation and full democratisation process following the violent and bloody conflicts of the last decade ; points out that this is in accordance with the undertakings given by the governments of these States in the framework of the Association and Stabilisation Process ; 2 Calls on the authorities of Serbia and Montenegro to closely monitor the situation and to take the measures necessary to ensure that similar events do not happen again in the future ; 3 Asks its delegation for South East Europe and the Sub-Committee on Human Rights to closely monitor developments in the Voivodina province ; 4 Instructs its President to forward this resolution to the Council, the Commission, the Government of Serbia and Montenegro and the authorities of the Voivodina province.
